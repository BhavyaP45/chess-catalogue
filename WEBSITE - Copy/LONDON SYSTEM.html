The London System

The current world chess champion Magnus Carlsen of Norway Source: Wikipedia

Significance

The London System is a solid opening that can be played in almost any position, without regard for black's position. The main reason for this is that it is based around white's development and central control. The primary asset in the position is white's dark-squared bishop, as it holds a strong diagonal. Of course, no opening is perfect, meaning that this opening has some cons as well. Given the defensive nature of this opening, there is less pressure on black's position and thus, less chances for attack. In addition, the bishop on f4 is weak and can be easily traded, which can damage White's central pawn structure.

Interesting Facts:

  • On chess.com around 9,523 high level games were played with the London system
  • Magnus Carlsen, the current world champion, uses this opening as a part of his repertoire, despite the high likelihood of draws.

London System

This is a solid queen's pawn opening, which is why d4 must be played by white. This move takes control of the center and controls the squares d4 and e5.
