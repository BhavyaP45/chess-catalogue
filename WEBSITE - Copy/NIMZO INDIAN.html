The Nimzo-Indian Defence

Vishy Anand during the 2017 World Rapid and Blitz Championship Source: ChessBase India

Significance

The Nimzo-Indian defence is one of the best openings for black, as white only wins 34-37% of the time when certain fourth moves are played. It is a great response to 1. d4, where black gives up the bishop pair for rapid development. The major cons however is that white can try and conquer the center, since black's first moves are often passive.

Interesting Facts:

  • Multiple fourth moves have been tried against the Nimzo-Indian, with 9 becoming the most prominent
  • Two have become very popular, with over 25,000 appearances at high level games
  • Used in World Championship matches by Bobby Fischer, Gary Kasparov, Vishy Anand and Magnus Carlsen

The Nimzo-Indian
