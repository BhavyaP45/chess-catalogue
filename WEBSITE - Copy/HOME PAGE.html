Introduction

The game of chess depicts a war between two great armies. Each piece represents a different type of warrior, who all aid in protecting the king. Originating from India in the 6th century, this game has become a staple in the lives of many. The rapid rise in popularity can be attributed to an increase in chess content on social media, as well as new exposure to softwares such as Lichess and Chess.com. For beginners however, the complexity can leave many confused, with no leads on where to start their journey. My chess catalogue provides free resources to serve as a starting point for beginners who wish to learn about chess.

About the Initiative

When I started playing chess, it was challenging for me to improve, as I did not know where to direct my research. Figuring out which openings were suitable for my skill level took a lot of unnecessary time, as I had to filter out lots of information. As a solution for this problem, I wanted to create a website where people can get a brief overview on important lines of openings as well as useful terms/ideas. It essentially serves as a guide so that people can explore further in areas of their interest.

For my personal project, I decided to make this dream a reality. I spent my free time learning about two major concepts; coding and openings. To create the website itself, I learned both HTML and CSS, and libraries such as Bootstrap. For the interactive opening trainer, I learned Javascript to switch between frames.
