Importance of Openings

Openings are a sequence of moves at the beginning of a chess game. Essentially, these openings help players get an edge over their opponent in the early stages of the game. They essentially give them an aspect of control over the infinite possibilities of moves, and can ease into a familiar/advantageous middle game. It can be very useful to learn an opening so that there are more opportunities for counterplay in the future. Many popular openings at the beginner and intermediate level revolve around the basic principles of chess. The first is control over the central 4 squares; d4, d5, e4 and e5. The main reason for this is because these squares give the most possibilities for pieces to move, and can be used to start an attack on both the king and queen sides. Moreover, these openings help with piece development. Piece development is the action of moving the larger pieces, mainly the bishops and knights, off of their initial rank and giving them a more active position on the board. Doing this early on in the game prevents enemy infiltration into the position, and allows for castling the king to safety.
