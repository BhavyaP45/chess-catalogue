The Caro Kann Defence

Jose Raul Capablanca playing chess in 1930. Source: Chess.com

Significance

The Caro Kann defence is referred to as a safe and solid opening. Black has a stable central structure, and frees up the light-squared bishop for development. The main flaw is that black has less space, as they play the passive move c6. However, understanding the lines can help mitigate this disadvantage and give black a solid start to the game.

Interesting Facts:

  • Around 110,007 games were played with this opening at high levels on chess.com
  • Got famous in the 20th century when world champion Jose Raul Capablanca used it as his main weapon against e4

The Caro Kann

White must play e4, which is one of the most common opening moves. At higher levels, this has a highest draw rate than d4, as the positions are more balanced.

1. e4

Black responds with c6, setting up d5 in the future. There are many different variations that can take place from this point forward.
